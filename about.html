I was trained to see systems — but living and working across multiple cultures taught me that form follows feeling as much as it follows function.

My practice doesn't fit one category. I move between spatial design, visual direction, research and experimentation — because the most interesting work happens at intersections. Immersing myself in radically different environments, climates and architectural traditions gave me something no single curriculum could: the ability to read a place, and respond to it on its own terms.

I've worked across scales — from a coffeehouse interior to a 32,000 m² masterplan — and across disciplines, from concept sketches to post-production, from functional zoning to brand positioning. I navigate the full span because I believe that architects who only know architecture are only half-equipped.

My personal practice — video, custom clothing, photography, collage — isn't separate from the work. It's how I process atmosphere, test sensation, and stay honest about what spaces actually feel like to inhabit. Architecture was always just one of the languages.

I don't separate designing a building from crafting an image. Whether it's a hillside villa, a cultural institution or a film — the question is the same: does this resonate? Does it imprint? Does it connect a person to their environment?
