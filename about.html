Anastasiya Vasileuskaya

I believe a space isn't truly complete until it's felt. My work lives at the intersection of architecture, art, and narrative—a search for the emotional core of the places we inhabit.

Growing up within Slavic visual culture, I was driven to understand how environment shapes experience. This led me to study and work across Southern Europe, immersing myself in its light, textures, and traditions. Living and creating across these contexts taught me that powerful design isn't imposed—it's discovered through deep observation and a willingness to be reshaped by your surroundings.

This global perspective became my foundation. I've spent years in multidisciplinary environments, collaborating on projects that blur lines between architecture, interiors, master planning, and brand strategy. I navigate from concept to construction, always seeing the systemic whole.

But architecture was never my only language. My personal practice—video, collage, photography, custom clothing—isn't a separate hobby. It's how I explore the memories spaces hold, the atmospheres they project, the stories they tell when no one speaks.

I don't separate designing a building from crafting an image. Whether it's a tropical villa, a master plan, or a film about an abandoned house, my goal is the same: to create work that resonates, that imprints itself, that connects people to their environment.

I believe the more you explore, the more you discover. I'm here to experiment, to blur lines, and to build worlds that matter—physical and imagined.
